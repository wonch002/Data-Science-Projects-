Overview¶

Given a dataset that describes customer purchasing patterns and behaivors, we want form clusters that describe different types of customers. Using these clustered customers, this grocery store can develop marketing campaigns and deals that appeal to these specfic types of customers.

Data¶

This data set describes customer data of a grocery store distributor in Portugal.

The following variables are present in the data set:

  1. FRESH - annual spending (m.u.) on fresh products (Continuous);
  2. MILK - annual spending (m.u.) on milk products (Continuous);
  3. GROCERY - annual spending (m.u.)on grocery products (Continuous);
  4. FROZEN - annual spending (m.u.)on frozen products (Continuous)
  5. DETERGENTS_PAPER - annual spending (m.u.) on detergents and paper products (Continuous)
  6. DELICATESSEN - annual spending (m.u.)on and delicatessen products (Continuous);
  7. CHANNEL - customers Channel - Horeca (Hotel/Restaurant/Caf) (Represented as 1) or Retail channel (Represented as 2) (Nominal)
  8. REGION - customers Region Lisnon (Represented as 1), Oporto (Represented as 2) or Other (Represented as 3) (Nominal)

Intertia¶
Inertia will tell us how far points are from the center of the cluster. That is, it sums up all the distances of all points within a cluster to the center of that cluster.


This should intuively tell us that the smaller our Interia, the better our clustering.
